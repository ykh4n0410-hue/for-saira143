What I need to say

I need to say this properly. What I did was wrong. Raising my voice at you and embarrassing you in front of your friends is something you never deserved. I know it wasn’t just an argument. It was a moment where I hurt someone I love, and I understand why it damaged your trust in me. Seeing how much it affected you, knowing that I made you cry and made you feel like you didn’t even want to speak to me, has stayed with me. I wish I could take that moment back, but I can’t. All I can do is be honest about it and take responsibility for it. I’m not sorry because you’re upset with me. I’m sorry because I genuinely hurt you. I failed to treat you with the respect, care, and patience that you deserve, especially in front of other people. When I think about it, what hurts me most is knowing that I became the reason you felt embarrassed and hurt when I should have been the person making you feel safe, respected, and supported no matter who was around us. I know trust isn’t rebuilt by words. If it was, I would say sorry a thousand times and everything would be fixed. But I understand that trust comes back through actions, consistency, and time. I don’t expect one message to make things okay, and I don’t expect you to suddenly stop being angry. You have every right to feel the way you do, and I accept that. What I hope you know is that the worst moment of my behavior is not the whole of who I am. What I did was real, and I take full responsibility for it, but I am determined to prove through my actions that I can be better than that moment. I don’t want to just tell you I’ve changed. I want to show you, day by day, in the way I speak to you, respect you, and treat you. When I think about us, I think about the day before Christmas entertainment when we were both so excited. I think about the moment we both realised we liked each other. I think about our first ever call and those Morrison’s Disney trading cards. Those memories are some of my favourite memories, and they remind me of how special you are to me. They remind me of how naturally happy you make me and how lucky I felt knowing that someone as amazing as you liked me back. That is why this hurts so much. Not because I’m facing the consequences of my actions, but because I hurt someone who means so much to me. You have brought so much happiness, comfort, laughter, and love into my life, and the fact that I made you feel the opposite of that breaks my heart. I can’t ask you to forget what happened, but I hope that with time I can earn back the trust I lost. Not because I’m entitled to another chance, but because you deserve a version of me that learns from his mistakes and grows from them. No matter how long it takes, I’m willing to put in the effort to prove that this moment does not define how much I love you, how much I respect you, or the kind of partner I want to be for you. I never want you to feel the way I made you feel that day again. You mean more to me than my pride, more to me than being right, and more to me than any argument we could ever have. I know I let you down, and I know saying sorry doesn’t undo the hurt. But I promise that I will spend my time showing you through my actions that the boy who raised his voice at you that day is not the man I want to be for you. I love you, I miss seeing you happy, and I am truly sorry.

Love from,

Yasir aka Mr drooler ❤️
